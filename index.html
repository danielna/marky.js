Usage

  1. Drag the "marky" bookmarklet to your bookmarks toolbar.
  2. When reading a long page that you'd like to mark positions in, click the "marky" bookmarklet to instantiate marky.
  3. Scroll to the desired place to mark. Click the "Mark!" button and enter a name in the textbox. Press enter to save the mark.
  4. If you click the "Mark!" button accidentally click it again to close the textbox.
  5. To navigate to a mark, select it from the right side of the screen. Marks are positioned at heights that are relative to their position within the entire document.
  6. To delete a mark, select the [x] near the mark name.
  7. Click the bookmarklet again to hide/show marky from the screen.

Background

Note: this library actually has nothing to do with Marky Mark or Mark Wahlberg. It's a bookmarklet that needed a name.

Marky.js is a bookmarklet that will mark your current location on a long page. That location will be stored in your browser using the HTML5 LocalStorage API, so there's no worry of the NSA tracking your reading progress through wikipedia.

This is useful when doing things like keeping track of progress through extended, single-page programming tutorials, which is the specific problem I'm writing this bookmarklet to fix.

This bookmarklet is an exploration of bookmarklets, jQuery-less DOM manipulation, Coffeescript and LocalStorage.

This should work in Chrome and Firefox. If you're working in IE YMMV, like it will with the rest of the Internet.
